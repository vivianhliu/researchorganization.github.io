Resources

Everything you need to find research opportunities and succeed in your scientific journey.

💡 Email Tips
1
Try to keep your emails under thirteen sentences.
2
Read recently published articles by the professor and personalize your email using what you learned.
3
Include a specific question you have or idea for their research.
4
Ask to meet in person to discuss their research and potential lab positions.
5
Always clarify that you are a TAMS student.

📄 Email Template

Subject: TAMS Student Interested in [Research Topic] - Request for Lab Position Dear Dr. [Professor's Last Name], My name is [Your Name], and I am a [Junior/Senior] at the Texas Academy of Mathematics and Science at the University of North Texas. I am writing to express my strong interest in your research on [specific topic from their recent publication]. I recently read your paper "[Paper Title]" published in [Journal Name], and I was particularly fascinated by [specific finding or methodology]. This sparked my curiosity about [specific question related to their work]. I am eager to contribute to your lab and would welcome the opportunity to discuss potential research positions. I am available to meet at your convenience, either in person or virtually. Thank you for considering my request. I look forward to hearing from you. Best regards, [Your Name] TAMS Class of [Year] [Your Email]

🔎 UNT Research Database

Search by keyword in UNT research databases. Start with a professor's research interest, then filter results by reading their publications.

↗ UNT Research Portal ↗ UNT Experts Database
📘 Pro Tip

When reaching out to professors, mention specific papers they've published and explain why their research interests you. This shows genuine interest and increases your chances of getting a response.

✅ What gets replies

Personalization + a clear ask. Say what you read, what you’re curious about, and what you want (meeting, lab spot, or advice).

⏱ Follow-up timing

If you don’t hear back, wait 5–7 days and send a short follow-up with one sentence of new info (a paper you read, updated availability).

🧠 Quick structure

1) Who you are + TAMS. 2) Why their work. 3) One specific question. 4) A clear ask. 5) Availability.
